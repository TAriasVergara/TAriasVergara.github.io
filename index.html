About

I received a B.Sc. in Electronic Engineering from the University of Antioquia (UdeA, Colombia) in 2014, a Master of Science degree at the same institution in 2017, and a Ph.D. in Computer Science (summa cum laude) in 2022 through a bi-national joint program between UdeA and FAU. Since 2023 I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Pattern Recognition Lab (FAU, Erlangen).

Since 2015 my research has focused on speech processing and machine learning methods for the analysis of pathological speech signals resulting from neurological (e.g., Parkinson's disease), structural (e.g., children with cleft lip and palate), and perceptual (e.g., hearing loss) disorders. I have also investigated the effect of the natural aging process on speech, contributed to Android-based applications for collecting and analyzing data from Parkinson's disease patients and adults and children with hearing loss, and developed automatic methods for the analysis of high-speed videoendoscopy data of people with voice disorders. More recently my work extends to multimodal medical imaging, including cine-MRI of speech production in tongue cancer patients.
